Puto Binan (Laguna)

Description

Puto Binan is a popular rice cake from Laguna. It's often topped with melted butter, condensed milk, grated cheese, and salted egg, making it a delightful and sweet treat.

History

Puto Binan has a rich history in the town of Biñan, Laguna. It was originally created by a housewife named Petronila Samaniego. Over the years, this delicious rice cake has become a beloved tradition and is passed down from generation to generation.

Suggested Retail Price

Plain: ₱150-₱160
Salted: ₱170-₱180

Ingredients

  • Plain rice flour
  • Sugar
  • Baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • Coconut milk
  • Toppings:
  • Melted butter
  • Condensed milk
  • Cheddar or gouda cheese
  • Salted egg

Procedure

  • Prepare a steamer with a container that can fit the batter.
  • If necessary, divide the batter into two containers.
  • Grease the container(s) with coconut oil.
  • Mix rice flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and coconut milk in a bowl.
  • Let the batter rest for 30 minutes, then mix again.
  • Cover the container(s) with a towel-wrapped lid to absorb condensation.
  • Steam for 15 minutes until the puto is springy to touch.
