Sinapot (Bicol)

Description

Sinapot is a fried banana dessert that is in some ways similar to turon. It is made from Saba or Cardaba banana. The fruit is peeled then sliced horizontally before being mixed in a batter consisting of flour and eggs. After being deep fried, it is ready to be serve.

History

Sinapot literally translates to Dinikit-dikit or sticky in Tagalog / Filipino language.

Suggested Retail Price

The price range about Php 8.00 to Php 15.00 per 4 piece banana.

Ingredients

  • 8 pieces ripe banana, saba 1 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • oil for frying

Procedure

  • In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, milk, beaten egg and water.
  • Slightly beat all the ingredients to mix.
  • Add the salt and sugar and beat until it form a batter. Set aside.
  • Prepare the ripe saba by peeling it and sliced each piece in 2 length wise cut.
  • Put the sliced bananas in the batter mixture and make sure all sides are coated with the batter.
  • Heat oil in pan over medium heat and fry the banana for about 10â€“15 minutes or until golden brown.
  • Drain excess oil using paper towels.
