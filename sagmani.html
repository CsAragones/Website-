Sagmani (Leyte)

Description

Sagmani is cooked from either cassava,sweet potatoes, gabi or mixture of any. The tradition gata or coconut milk is the Visayan ambrosia that completes this dish. It is then added with sugar, coconut meat, and nuts which is one of the delicacy's key ingredients.

History

Sagmani is one of the popular pasalubong delicacies from Leyte, where you can find "Aida's Delicacies", "Terry Leyte Delicacies", "Delicacies de Ynego". These are lots of native delicacies in their stall at affordable price .

Suggested Retail Price

3pcs of sagmani is Php200

Ingredients

  • 4 cups grated cassava
  • 2 cups grated coconut
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Banana leaves for wrapping
  • Cooking oil for greasing

Procedure

  • In a large bowl, combine the grated cassava, grated coconut, coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Mix well until fully incorporated.
  • Prepare the banana leaves by passsing them over an open flame to make them pliable. Wipe clean and set aside.
  • Grease one side of each banana leaf with cooking oil.
  • Place about 1/2 cup of the cassava mixture onto the greased side of the banana leaf. Fold the leaf.Fold the leaf over the mixture and seal the edges, forming a rectangular packet.
  • Repeat with the remaining cassava mixture and banana leaves.
  • Steam the wrapped sagmani packets for 30-45 minutes, or until the cassava is cooked through and firms to the touch.
  • Remove the sagmani packets from the steamer and allow them to cool slight before unwarpping and servingss.
