Sagmani (Leyte)

Description

Puto LansonPuto lanson is one common delicacy made from kamoteng-kahoy or cassava. It is easy to prepare and it doesn't really need too many ingredients.

History

Puto Lanson is a puto variety which originates from the Western Visayas region particularly in Iloilo City. Originally, it is called Aripahol Balinghoy.

Suggested Retail Price

P10.00 per piece

Ingredients

  • 4 cups grated cassava
  • 6 tbsp. brown sugar
  • 1 cup grated Young Coconut (long and thin)
  • Mascuvado (optional)
  • Tin Molds ( you can use Condensed Milk can, cut in half and make sure to put holes at the bottom )

Procedure

  • Prepare the Cassava, peel and wash it.
  • Grate the Cassava once finished then squeeze out the juice using cheese cloth, air dried.
  • Mix the grated cassava with brown sugar and grated coconut altogether.
  • Stuff them in your Tin molds and steam. If the Tin molds are not available you can use Muffin pan or wrap it with banana leaves as substitute.
  • Steam for about 15 to 25 minutes.
  • Once cooked you can spread butter or margarine, some like it with a little Mascuvado on top.
  • Serve it hot with Hot Choco drink or Coffee.
